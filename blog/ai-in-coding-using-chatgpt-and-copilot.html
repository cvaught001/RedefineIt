Why these tools matter

ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot boost throughput and exploration. They’re most valuable when used to unstick yourself, scaffold boilerplate, and quickly explore alternative approaches — not to replace judgment.

Effective use cases

  • Drafting boilerplate, scaffolds, and test harnesses
  • Refactoring suggestions and code review prompts
  • Generating docs, comments, and migration checklists
  • API usage examples and framework‑specific patterns
  • “Rubber‑ducking” complex problems with structured prompts

Prompt discipline

  • State the goal, constraints, and context (language, framework, versions).
  • Break work into small, verifiable steps with examples.
  • Iterate with feedback; keep the conversation anchored to code.

Quality, safety, and licensing

  • Always compile, test, and lint AI‑assisted code.
  • Guard your secrets and private data; review privacy settings.
  • Be mindful of license compatibility and attribution policies.

Team patterns

  • Use AI as a pair — not an authority. Treat output as a draft.
  • Adopt lightweight guidelines (where to use, where not to).
  • Track outcomes: PR cycle time, defect rates, and onboarding velocity.

Bottom line

AI copilots accelerate the mechanics of coding and help you explore solution space faster. The best results come from clear prompting, incremental verification, and steady engineering standards.
