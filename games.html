Games — Interactive Play and Nostalgia

I’ve always loved the spirit of early games — that sense of wonder packed into a few pixels and sounds. Much of my creative energy goes into building retro-inspired games that echo the feel of 1980s arcades and the early days of Flash animation. They’re simple, colorful, and full of motion — made to capture the joy and immediacy of play.

I work in a very iterative way: starting with small sketches in JavaScript, experimenting with physics, timing, and sound, and then slowly expanding the rules into more complete worlds. It’s a process that feels a lot like those early computers I grew up on — creative constraint that invites invention.

Over the years, I’ve built several web-based experiments and a collection of Flash games that I’m now refactoring to run natively, without plugins — preserving that retro energy for the modern web.

Each project is both a memory and a new experiment — an attempt to recreate the feeling of sitting in front of a glowing screen, joystick in hand, watching pixels come to life.
