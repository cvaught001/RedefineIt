Kinetic Art and Animation — Where Math Becomes Motion

A Beautiful Mind (2001) is a biographical drama about mathematician John Nash, a man who saw patterns hidden within the chaos of everyday life. That idea has always resonated with me. I’ve long been fascinated by the symmetry of geometry, the rhythm of trigonometry, and the beauty that appears when mathematics becomes visual.

When my father brought home an IBM PC with a monochrome monitor, I spent hours trying to make something move on the screen. Then came the Commodore 64, and everything changed. The Commodore 64 demo scene revealed what creativity through constraint could be — glowing raster bars, scrolling text, and sprite multiplexers that felt like pure magic.

It was art built from timing, math, and imagination — code as performance — and it shaped how I think about creation to this day. That same fascination still drives me: exploring kinetic art, interactive animation, and generative visuals where geometry, sound, and code move as one.
