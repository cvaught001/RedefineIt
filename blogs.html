Fibonacci Flow — Spirals in Motion

By Christopher Vaught • Oct 2025 • 6 min read

I’ve always been fascinated by the Fibonacci sequence — the way simple ratios appear in shells, plants, and galaxies. This study turns that math into motion: drawing arcs, rectangles, and golden angle steps to reveal living patterns.

The first prototype mapped squares to a growing grid and animated bezier arcs between them. The remix focused on smoothing the camera and using subtle color drift to better suggest depth. Audio hooks let the rhythm lock to growth steps and create a meditative pace.

The fun part is how tiny changes ripple: adjust the angle a few degrees, add easing to radius growth, or modulate hue by step — and a new family of spirals appears.
